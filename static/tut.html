What is Data Science?

Data science, in its most basic terms, can be defined as obtaining insights and information, really anything of value, out of data.

Like any new field, it's often tempting but counterproductive to try to put concrete bounds on its definition. In reality, Data Science is evolving so fast and has already shown such enormous range of possibility that a wider definition is essential to understand it.

And while it's hard to pin down a specific definition, it's quite easy to see and feel its impact. Data science, when applied to different fields can lead to incredible new insights. And the folks that are using it are already reaping the benefits





And we know that it’s hard to imagine a world before data.

What is Machine Learning?

Machine learning (or just ML) is probably the most hyped yet most interesting part of data science.

Data scientists define Machine Learning as the process of using machines ( computers) to better understand a process or system, and recreate, replicate or augment that system. In other words, Machines process data and develop new systems for understanding it. These methods are often based around that fancy buzzword “algorithms” we hear so much when folks talk about Google or Amazon.




In technical terms, the most common divisions are Supervised, Unsupervised, and Reinforcement Learning.

Types of Machine Learning and their Types


Supervised Learning

Supervised learning is a type of ML where the model is provided with labeled training data. But what does that mean?

For example, suppose you are an budding botanist determined to differentiate between two species of the Lilliputian plant genus (a completely made-up plant). The two species look pretty similar. Fortunately, a botanist has put together a dataset of Lilliputian plants she found in the wild along with their species name.

The training data includes both Inputs and Labels also called Targets

All the Supervised Learning problems can be put under either of the categories:

Classification

Regression

Unsupervised Learning

In unsupervised learning, the goal is to identify meaningful patterns in the data. To accomplish this, the machine must learn from an unlabeled data set. In other words, the model has no hints how to categorize each piece of data and must infer its own rules for doing so.
